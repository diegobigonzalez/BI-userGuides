üìà Volume Distribution

The left side of the dashboard displays two donut charts showing volume distribution:

üè¶ Total Traded Volumes per Bank

Shows the breakdown of total traded volume by each liquidity provider. Quickly identify which banks handle the majority of your FX volume and their relative market share.

üí± Total Traded Volumes by Currency Pair

Displays volume distribution across different currency pairs (EUR/USD, EUR/GBP, EUR/MXN, etc.). Understand which currency pairs dominate your trading activity.

üí° Interactive Feature: Click on any bank or currency pair segment to filter the entire dashboard. You can also select multiple items to create custom combinations.

üìã Wallet Shares Analysis

Three matrix tables show how trading volume is distributed across banks from different perspectives:

Wallet Shares % by Tenor

Shows the percentage of volume each bank captures at different tenors (time to maturity). Rows represent tenors (1-Year, 2-Year, etc. or 1-Month, 2-Months depending on grouping), and columns represent banks.

How to Read

If Bank A shows 45% in the "2-Year" row, it means 45% of all 2-year tenor trades were executed with Bank A. Row totals always sum to 100%.

Wallet Shares % by Currency Pair

Displays bank distribution for each currency pair traded. Useful for identifying if certain banks are more competitive in specific currency pairs.

‚ö†Ô∏è Insight Example: If Bank A normally wins most trades but shows low share in EUR/GBP, it may indicate Bank A isn't competitive in that specific pair - an opportunity to negotiate better rates.

Wallet Shares % by Amount Bins

Trades are categorized into Low, Mid, and High value buckets based on trade size. This reveals if certain banks are more competitive for different trade sizes.

Dynamic Buckets

The Low, Mid, and High thresholds are dynamically calculated based on each client's trading patterns. Hover over the "?" icon in the dashboard to see the specific ranges for your selected client.

üîç Analysis Tip: If a bank wins most low-value trades but loses high-value ones, they may not be competitive when larger amounts are at stake - consider challenging them on pricing for bigger trades.

üìâ Spread Analysis

The spread represents the difference between the bank's quoted rate and the market rate. Understanding spread patterns helps identify the true cost of trading with each provider.

AVG Spread by Liquidity Provider

A line chart showing the average spread evolution for each bank over time (grouped by months or years). Each colored line represents a different liquidity provider.

  • ‚Ä¢
    X-Axis: Time period (months or years depending on Tenor Grouping selection)
  • ‚Ä¢
    Y-Axis: Spread percentage
  • ‚Ä¢
    Lines: Each bank is represented by a different color
üí° Key Insight: Spreads typically increase with longer tenors (more time = more uncertainty). Compare how each bank's spread curve behaves - a bank with relatively flat spread at longer tenors may offer better value for forward contracts.
‚ö†Ô∏è What to Watch: If a bank's spread suddenly increases at certain tenors while others remain stable, it may indicate they're less competitive for those maturities.
